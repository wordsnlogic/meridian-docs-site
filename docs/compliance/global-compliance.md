# Global Compliance

Meridian maintains compliance certifications across a broad set of regulatory frameworks, enabling you to accept payments globally without building your own compliance infrastructure.

## Core certifications

| Certification | Scope |
|---|---|
| **PCI DSS Level 1** | Cardholder data security — global |
| **SOC 2 Type II** | Security, availability, and confidentiality — US |
| **ISO 27001** | Information security management — global |
| **GDPR** | Data protection for EU customers |

## Regional licenses and registrations

| Region | Status |
|---|---|
| United States | Money Transmitter Licenses in all required states |
| European Union | Licensed as a Payment Institution under PSD2 |
| United Kingdom | FCA-authorized Payment Institution |
| Canada | Registered with FINTRAC as an MSB |
| Australia | AUSTRAC-registered |
| Singapore | Licensed by MAS |

## Data residency

By default, customer data may be processed and stored in any Meridian data region. For businesses with strict data residency requirements, Meridian offers **regional data isolation** — contact sales for availability.

## GDPR and data protection

Meridian acts as a **data processor** when processing payments on your behalf. You are the data controller. Meridian's Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is available at **Dashboard → Settings → Compliance → DPA**.

Key points:
- Customer card data is encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.2+)
- Meridian does not sell customer data to third parties
- Data subject requests (access, deletion) can be submitted via the Dashboard

## Strong Customer Authentication (SCA)

For EU and UK transactions, Meridian automatically handles **Strong Customer Authentication** requirements under PSD2. This typically means triggering 3D Secure 2.0 for qualifying transactions. Meridian exemptions engine automatically applies eligible exemptions (low-value, low-risk, merchant-initiated) to minimize authentication friction.
